Viking Flatbread

Golden-brown flatbread on a wooden cutting board

Viking flatbread, a mainstay in Norse cuisine, is a straightforward and robust creation. Composed of whole wheat and barley flours, this unleavened bread is a testament to Viking resourcefulness. Cooked on griddles, it served as a versatile accompaniment to their meals, often enjoyed with smoked fish or honey. Its slightly sweet, nutty flavor and rustic texture make it a simple yet hearty delight that transports us back to the culinary traditions of the Viking age.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups (240g) whole wheat flour
  • 1 cup (120g) barley flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 cup (237ml) water

Steps

  1. Mix the Dry Ingredients
  2. Add Honey
  3. Gradually Add Water
  4. Knead the Dough
  5. Rest the Dough
  6. Divide and Roll
  7. Cook on a Griddle
  8. Serve Warm and enjoy
